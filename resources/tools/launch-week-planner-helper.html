Notes
  • Press keys need more lead time than most teams give them. Three weeks before launch sounds like plenty. In practice, a journalist needs time to play the game and file the piece before the embargo. If your game runs longer than 5 to 6 hours, send keys earlier, sometimes 4 to 5 weeks out for a narrative game or an RPG. This timeline uses 21 days as a baseline. Move it to fit your game's length and genre.
  • Line up the embargo lift and the trailer. When you run a review embargo, push the trailer and your big marketing beats live the moment the embargo lifts. Drop the trailer early, then go quiet while reviews sit under embargo, and you bleed your own momentum. With no embargo, send the trailer 7 to 10 days before launch so the algorithm has time to work.
  • A day-one patch is a launch decision, so make it before launch week. If you know a real fix is coming, plan the patch window now. A patch that lands 3 days after launch still catches most of the review traffic. A patch that lands 2 weeks out misses it. Decide this in advance, not in the middle of launch day.
  • Treat this as an estimate. Real dates move with your genre, team size, and platform mix. Use it as a planning scaffold, then confirm the hard dates against your own production plan.
